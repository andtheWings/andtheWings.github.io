If you only use baseline parole/probation status to predict 3-month retention, the coefficient is -0.62 (p = 0.01). Being on parole or probation decreases the odds of retention by -46% (OR = 0.54).

If you control with ASI drug composite score and having ever injected drugs, the coefficient for parole/probation drops to -0.55 (p = 0.03). In the controlled model, being on parole or probation decreases the odds of retention by -43% (OR = 0.56).
